# VISION.md

## Current Vision

### Project: NYC Restaurant Inspection Risk Prediction

**Primary Stakeholder:** Restaurant owners and managers in NYC who want to proactively prepare for health inspections

**Secondary Stakeholders:** 
- NYC Department of Health (inspection planning and transparency)
- Customers (indirectly benefit from improved compliance and safety)
- Restaurant groups and chains (operational oversight across locations)

**Problem Statement:**
Restaurant owners in New York City often lack clear, data-driven insight into when their establishments are likely to be inspected. While inspection results and grades are publicly available, there is limited guidance on inspection timing or patterns. This makes it difficult for restaurants to proactively prepare, increasing the risk of poor inspection outcomes and compliance issues.

**Solution:**
We build a machine learning model that predicts the likelihood of a restaurant being inspected within a given time frame based on historical inspection data, violation patterns, and temporal trends. The model provides a probability score that helps restaurant owners anticipate inspections and take proactive steps to maintain compliance.

## Version History

### Version 1.0 - Initial Vision

**Project: NYC Restaurant Inspection Outcome Prediction**

**Primary Stakeholder:** NYC Department of Health inspectors

**Problem Statement:**
Inspectors could benefit from predictive tools to identify restaurants most likely to receive poor inspection scores or violations, allowing for more targeted inspections.

**Solution:**
Build a model that predicts inspection outcomes, including grades and violation severity, to support more efficient inspection targeting.

**Change Reason:**  
After feedback and further analysis, we shifted the focus of the project:
1. Predicting inspection outcomes primarily benefits inspectors and may reinforce existing enforcement biases  
2. The stakeholder focus was adjusted to support restaurant owners rather than regulators  
3. Predicting inspection likelihood better aligns with proactive compliance and prevention  
4. The revised approach encourages improved behavior and preparation rather than reactive enforcement
